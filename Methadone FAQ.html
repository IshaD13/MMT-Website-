Answers to Frequently Asked Questions

For whom is the Methadone Maintenace Treatment made for?

The Methadone Maintenance Treatment is made for Heroin addicts to recover from their addiction. There can also be some patients who are called "Priority Patience" who are recommended to begin their treatment in the following 24 hours. Some of these patients are: those who test HIV positive, are receiving medication for Hepatitis C or HIV, have a history of drug overdose, pregnant, or have an opioid dependency.

Methadone vs Heroin

Methadone and Heroin share many similarities and differences, for example, both are full opioids, but Methadone is a partial-agonist while Heroin is a full agonist. to be and agonist means to be a substance that binds to a receptor and alters the receptor state. Heroin also produces an euphoria or a "high", but Methadone doesn't and actually counters the euphoric effects of other opioids.

Methadone vs Buprenorphine

Both Methadone and Buprenorphine are opioids, meaning that they activate the opioid (mu) receptors on nerve cells. Methadone is a full agonist and Buprenorphine is a partial agonist. Methadone continues to to produce effect on receptors until either the maximum effect is reached or all receptors are fully activated, but Buprenorphine does not activate the mu receptors to the same degree due to only being a partial agonist; the effects of Buprenorphine stop increasing after reaching a plateau.
