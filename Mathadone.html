Heroin Related Deaths Issue

The rising Heroin Epidemic is a critical problem which needs to be contained as soon as possible. There are many causes of this heroin epidemic, and a large sum comes from the misuse of pain-relievers as 80% of heroin addicts first misused prescription medication and then moved to a more powerful and euphoric drug. This may have started in the 1990s when pharmaceutical companies claims that their pain reliever are not addictive, so doctors began to prescribe them more carelessly, making people addicted to painkillers (“Opioid Overdose Crisis”). There have been many solutions proposed such as: improving access to treatment and recovery centres, promoting use of overdose-reversing drugs, advancing better methods for pain management, but Most importantly creating treatments such as Methadone Maintenance Treatment.

What is Methadone?

Methadone Maintenance Treatment uses Methadone for a prolonged treatment for Opioid addicts (such as heroin addicts) by substituting an Heroin, a very harmful and addictive drug with Methadone, a much safer option. Methadone is a synthetic opioid medication which is mostly used to solve opioid addiction, but can also be used to treat chronic pain. Methadone is a full agonist opioid, so it efficiently imitates other opioids by fully activating the receptors in the brain. Methadone attaches to the receptors in the brain, and at an appropriate dose it does not create the euphoria or a “high” like other opioids do. Methadone helps with the cravings for opioids, reducing any chances of relapses, but does not eliminate any withdrawal symptoms when it is out of the system. It helps controlling the withdrawal symptoms and cravings, and has some positive opioid blocking qualities, meaning that it blocks the effects of other opioids when Methadone is present in the person’s symptoms (“Methadone”).

*Opioid: substances which act on opioid receptors in our brain to produce produce a euphoria or a morphine like effect
*Full agonist: an substance which creates the same euphoria or effect as an opioid
